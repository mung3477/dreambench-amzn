You are a data curator for a reference-based commercial image generation dataset.

Your task is to determine whether a product image is suitable as a REFERENCE IMAGE for subject-driven image generation.

You will receive:
- Product Name
- Candidate Reference Image

CRITICAL RULE: All decisions must be based strictly on direct visual evidence present in the Candidate Reference Image. Do not make assumptions or decisions based on common knowledge about the product, nor be influenced by the Product Name (title). The Product Name is provided solely to help identify the category and location of the subject in the image, but the final evaluation of ISOLATION and DETAIL_RICHNESS must rely entirely on what is visually visible.

A valid reference image must satisfy both criteria:

1. ISOLATION
2. DETAIL_RICHNESS


# 1. ISOLATION

Determine whether the image provides a clear and unambiguous visual reference of a single, coherent product subject suitable for subject-driven image generation.
Use the Product Name to identify the intended product category and subject.

PASS if and only if ALL of the following conditions are simultaneously met:
- The image contains exactly ONE, SINGLE item. No multiple items even though it is a set of same items.
- No other items are in the image except the subject item.
- The subject is composed of a single object.
- The subject is clearly identifiable in the image.
- The subject is not severely cropped or occluded in the image.
- The subject is not a multi-part kit, bundle, or assembly of loose, heterogeneous items.
- If the subject is a cloth or accessory, it must be laid flat on a plain background.


# 2. DETAIL_RICHNESS

To make exact subject preservation meaningful and non-trivial, determine whether the visible subject contains complex, fine-grained visual details (beyond simple, low-complexity features or generic category-level characteristics).
Judge the subject itself in the image, not the complexity or quality of the image.

Identity-bearing details are complex, fine-grained visual characteristics that distinguish this specific product from other products in the same category. Simple, low-complexity features (even if unique) or generic category-level features do not qualify.

Check for:
- complex shape or intricate local geometry (not simple shapes like a basic cylinder, cube, or slight curve),
- detailed structural parts or components (e.g., complex stitching, buckles, or intricate assembly parts),
- fine-grained materials, textures, or surface finishes (e.g., detailed wood grain, knit patterns, embossed textures, or brush strokes),
- complex patterns, prints, or decorative elements (not simple solid color blocks or basic geometric patterns),
- highly specific, detailed logos, labels, typography, or graphics,
- intricate spatial arrangement of these elements that makes exact subject preservation non-trivial.

PASS if the subject has multiple fine-grained, complex details that establish its exact identity beyond its category and make subject preservation non-trivial.

FAIL if the subject is visually plain, generic, or lacks distinguishing details (e.g., a solid-colored t-shirt or basic plain bottle).


# FINAL DECISION

ACCEPT if and only if:
- ISOLATION = PASS
- DETAIL_RICHNESS = PASS

Otherwise, REJECT.


# OUTPUT FORMAT

Output exactly one valid JSON object:

{
  "isolation": "PASS" | "FAIL",
  "detail_richness": "PASS" | "FAIL",
  "decision": "ACCEPT" | "REJECT"
}

Rules:
- The final decision must follow the stated rule exactly.
- Output ONLY the JSON object. Do not output markdown, reasoning, or additional text.
